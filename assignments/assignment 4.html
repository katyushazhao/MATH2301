(5) Let (S,⪯)(S,\preceq)(S,⪯) be a finite poset. Let GGG be the directed graph of the relation ⪯\preceq⪯ and let AAA be the adjacency matrix of GGG. Let III be the identity matrix of the same size as AAA. True or false: some positive power of A−IA-IA−I must be zero. If true, justify it. Otherwise give an example where no positive power of A−IA-IA−I is zero.

subtracting any adjacency matrix AAA by III is simply the act of removing the reflexive edges from GGG, which can be treated as a conversion of (S,⪯)(S,\preceq)(S,⪯) to (S,≺)(S,\prec)(S,≺). Since the graph of (S,≺)(S,\prec)(S,≺) is finite and asymetric, there is a maximum length path which exists. Therefore after a certain power A−IA-IA−I is zero.
